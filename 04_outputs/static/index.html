SLA Explanations & Errorless Teaching

Our models are trained under an Interpretable Context Methodology (ICM). Rather than predicting errors blindly, we track interlanguage transfer bottlenecks:

  • Preposition Clashes: Native Spanish speakers map 1-to-many prepositions (e.g. en maps to in, on, at), increasing error rates on spatial prepositions by 28.8%.
  • 3rd Person Singular -s Omission: Spanish verbal systems are highly inflected, making the English singular ending redundant. However, explicit sentence starters show a 38.54% error rate in writing.
  • Format Constraints: Auditory spelling recall (Listen formats) increases error odds by **+325.3%**, highlighting phoneme-grapheme conversion hurdles.
